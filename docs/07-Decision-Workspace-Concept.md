# Decision Workspace Concept

## Purpose

The Decision Workspace is the primary product experience for TradeEvidence. It is the place where a trader gathers market context, evaluates opportunities, examines evidence, considers risk, and prepares a decision.

Its purpose is not simply to display information. It exists to help the trader answer the next meaningful question: What should I be thinking about right now?

## Guiding Principles

The workspace should be shaped by the product's core principles:

- Market context comes first.
- Evidence should be clear and reviewable.
- Risks should be visible and explicit.
- Position sizing and capital allocation should be considered early.
- Every thesis should include an exit plan and invalidation criteria.
- The trader remains responsible for the final decision.
- The experience should grow from simple preparation toward richer analysis over time.

## Information Hierarchy

The workspace should organize information in a way that supports decision preparation:

1. Market Context
2. Market Statistics
3. Top Opportunities
4. Evidence
5. Risks
6. Pros & Cons
7. Position Sizing
8. Risk Focus
9. Thesis Invalidation
10. Journal

This order reflects the product's emphasis on understanding the environment before acting on a specific setup.

## Primary Widgets

The workspace should eventually include a set of core widgets that support the decision flow. The exact design is still evolving, but the following areas are expected to be central:

- Market context summary
- Watchlist or opportunity list
- Evidence cards or score summaries
- Risk and sizing panel
- Thesis and invalidation notes
- Journal entry prompt or post-decision reflection area
- AI decision-coach context panel

These widgets should remain modular so the experience can evolve from a curated workspace into a more customizable one.

## User Workflow

A typical user flow should look like this:

1. Review market context.
2. Identify the most relevant opportunities or areas of attention.
3. Examine supporting evidence and prior analysis.
4. Consider risks, trade-offs, and position sizing.
5. Record the thesis, exit plan, and assumptions.
6. Capture notes for later reflection and learning.

The workspace should make that flow feel natural and deliberate rather than crowded or overwhelming.

## Future Evolution

The Decision Workspace will evolve through the product's broader design roadmap:

- Version 1: a curated workspace that surfaces the most important context and next steps.
- Version 2: persona-based workspace templates that adapt the experience to different trading styles.
- Version 3: user-customizable dashboards built from widgets.

## Questions Still Under Consideration

The following design questions remain open and should be revisited as the product matures:

- How much information should be visible by default versus hidden behind progressive disclosure?
- Which widgets should be mandatory in the first release?
- How should the workspace support both quick review and deeper analysis?
- How should journaling and post-trade reflection be surfaced without becoming burdensome?
- How can AI support the workspace without replacing the trader's judgment?

Placeholder note: detailed interaction design, layout patterns, and component-level specifications will be defined in later design work.

## Desired Outcome

When a trader finishes using the workspace, they should be able to say:

- I understand the market.
- I understand the evidence.
- I understand the risks.
- I have a plan.
- I have earned confidence.
