# Trading Profile

## Purpose

The Trading Profile is the product's structured representation of a trader's context, preferences, and decision style. It helps TradeEvidence tailor education, workflows, templates, and AI explanation without changing the underlying market facts.

## Goals

The Trading Profile exists to help the product answer three practical questions:

- What kind of trader is this user?
- What guidance is most relevant to this user right now?
- How should the experience adapt without becoming intrusive or misleading?

## How It Differs from User Personas

User Personas describe broader audience segments and product positioning. The Trading Profile is a live, user-specific model that can evolve as the trader gains experience and changes approach.

In short:

- Personas help the product understand who the user may be.
- The Trading Profile helps the product adapt the experience for the specific user in front of it.

## Initial Onboarding Fields

The first version of the Trading Profile should capture the most important context needed for respectful personalization:

- experience level
- risk tolerance
- preferred trading style
- strategies of interest
- investment horizon
- primary goals
- constraints or preferences

These fields should be simple enough to complete quickly while still being meaningful.

## Future Profile Fields

As the product matures, the profile may expand to include:

- average position size
- typical holding period
- preferred market context (for example, swing, options, long-term investing, or active day trading)
- learning preferences
- tolerance for complexity
- frequency of review and journaling
- areas of recurring weakness or strength

## How the Profile Evolves

The Trading Profile should be treated as a living model rather than a one-time form.

It can evolve through:

- explicit user updates
- behavior patterns over time
- journal reflections
- saved templates or recurring workflow choices
- AI-assisted coaching prompts that clarify intent

## How Personalization Should Work

Personalization should be thoughtful and respectful. It should help users get relevant education, better workflow structure, and clearer explanations. It should never feel like the product is forcing a particular opinion.

The profile should influence:

- educational content and examples
- workflow templates
- AI explanations and framing
- suggested review prompts

## What Should Never Be Personalized

Market Context remains universal.

Facts never change.

The product should never personalize:

- market facts
- objective price data
- observed chart structure
- the existence of risk
- the reality of trade outcomes

Only education, workflows, templates, and AI explanations should adapt.

## Product Positioning

The Trading Profile should support better decision preparation, not a more opinionated product. It helps TradeEvidence become more relevant to the user while preserving neutrality, clarity, and user ownership.
