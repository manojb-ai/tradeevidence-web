# Decision Journal

## Vision

The Decision Journal preserves the reasoning behind a financial decision, not merely the resulting transaction. It supports preparation, review, reflection, learning, and celebration over time.

The journal is the long-term companion to the Decision Workspace. Where the workspace helps the trader prepare for a decision, the journal helps the trader remember what they thought, what changed, and what they learned.

## Evolution from MVP to Future Phases

### MVP — Decision Checklist
The first version of the journal experience begins inside the Decision Workspace as a “Before You Decide” checklist. It supports disciplined preparation without forcing the user into a heavy workflow.

### Phase 2 — Decision Snapshot
When a decision is saved, TradeEvidence can record an immutable snapshot of the context: market conditions, evidence, risks, position-sizing considerations, model and data versions, and the user's notes.

### Phase 3 — Decision Review
The journal then helps the user revisit the original thesis and compare it with current evidence. This makes it possible to understand what strengthened, what weakened, and whether the original invalidation criteria occurred.

### Phase 4 — Reflection and Learning
The journal can prompt reflection that separates outcome from process. The goal is to strengthen habits, not to defend a trade after the fact.

### Phase 5+ — Milestones and Decision Journey
The journal can eventually summarize milestones, investment anniversaries, learning themes, and personal growth. These features reinforce disciplined progress without turning the experience into a leaderboard.

## Core Components

### Decision Checklist
The checklist should be concise and practical. It can ask:

- Why am I interested?
- What supports the thesis?
- What contradicts it?
- What would invalidate it?
- Is the timing consistent with my horizon?
- Does the position size fit my risk rules?
- Have I considered the bearish case?
- Am I reacting to evidence or emotion?

MVP saving or printing is nice-to-have rather than mandatory.

### Decision Snapshot
A decision snapshot captures the state of the decision at the time it was formed. It can include:

- Date and time
- Market context
- Security context
- Evidence Score
- Decision Confidence
- Supporting evidence
- Counter-evidence
- Risks
- Invalidation criteria
- Position-sizing considerations
- User notes
- Model and data versions

The snapshot is immutable.

### Decision Review
TradeEvidence should help the user compare:

- Original thesis
- Current evidence
- What strengthened
- What weakened
- What remained unchanged
- Whether the original invalidation conditions occurred
- Whether the user would make the same decision today

### Reflection and Learning
Reflection should separate outcome from process. Helpful prompts include:

- What did I do well?
- What would I repeat?
- What would I change?
- Did I follow my position-sizing rules?
- Did I respond to evidence or emotion?
- What did the outcome teach me?

### Milestones
Milestones can celebrate profitable outcomes, disciplined behavior, patience, consistency, and learning. The emphasis should remain on growth and process rather than performance alone.

### Decision Journey
The long-term journey view can summarize:

- Decisions documented
- Reviews completed
- Disciplined decisions
- Common mistakes
- Most improved habits
- Learning themes
- Milestones
- Investment anniversaries

## AI Role

AI can help summarize changes between snapshots, ask reflection questions, identify recurring patterns, highlight adherence to the user's own rules, and generate private milestone summaries. It should support learning and self-awareness without judging the person or implying guaranteed outcomes.
