# Homepage Design Workshop

**Date:** 2026-07-13  
**Status:** Completed

## Executive Summary

The workshop synthesized the strongest elements from prior light and dark dashboard concepts and established the authenticated homepage as an inviting orientation and navigation experience.

The homepage should:

1. Explain today's market in plain language.
2. Give users a clear way to select their current intent.
3. Highlight a small number of relevant opportunities.
4. Provide compact supporting intelligence.
5. Make AI assistance prominent.
6. Avoid large tables and terminal-like density.

## Inputs Reviewed

### Dark-theme inspiration

- Compact sector heat map
- Clickable market-breadth categories

### Light-theme inspiration

- Market statistics
- Recent alerts
- Interesting setups
- Compact YES / WATCH / NO previews

### New prototype concepts

- Today's Market / Today's Briefing
- “What would you like to do today?”
- AI Coach / Ask TradeEvidence

## Homepage Information Hierarchy

### 1. Today's Briefing

Purpose: Orient the user.

Includes:

- Market environment
- Confidence
- Risk level
- Market regime
- Theme of the day
- Plain-English interpretation

### 2. What Would You Like to Do Today?

Purpose: Allow users to choose their immediate intent.

Potential actions:

- Research opportunities
- Review an existing position
- Review portfolio
- Learn today's market
- Journal and reflect
- Ask TradeEvidence

### 3. Highest Conviction Opportunities

Purpose: Show a small set of items worth reviewing.

Each item includes:

- Symbol
- Evidence score
- Plain-English reason
- Link to a focused workspace

### 4. Supporting Intelligence

Purpose: Provide useful context without overwhelming the user.

Components:

- Market statistics
- Interesting setups
- Compact sector heat map
- Recent alerts
- Compact breadth or category links

### 5. Prominent AI Panel

Purpose: Make education and guided analysis immediately accessible.

Decision:

- Use one AI experience.
- Remove duplicate AI Coach and AI Decision Coach entry points.
- Replace the oversized philosophy card with a large AI panel.

## Design Principles Confirmed

- Orient before presenting detail.
- Translate data into understanding.
- Homepage summarizes; workspaces provide detail.
- Large stock tables do not belong on the homepage.
- Supporting intelligence should remain compact.
- Market context remains universal.
- The user chooses the next activity.
