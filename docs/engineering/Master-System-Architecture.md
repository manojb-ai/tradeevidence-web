# Master System Architecture

**Status:** Approved

**Version:** 1.0

**Owner:** Chief Software Architect

---

# Purpose

This document defines the high-level software architecture for TradeEvidence.

It establishes the major architectural layers, subsystem boundaries, platform services, analytical engines, and the long-term evolution strategy.

It intentionally avoids implementation details such as database schema, API contracts, UI components, or deployment configuration.

Those are documented separately.

---

# Architectural Vision

TradeEvidence is a **Decision Intelligence Platform**.

The platform exists to help investors build **Earned Confidence** through evidence, disciplined evaluation, explainable reasoning, and continuous learning.

The system does not attempt to predict markets.

The system helps users make better decisions.

---

# Architectural Outcome

The architectural outcome of TradeEvidence is:

> Earned Confidence

Confidence is earned by:

- understanding market context
- evaluating evidence
- recognizing risk
- documenting reasoning
- reflecting on outcomes
- continuously improving future decisions

---

# Architecture Principles

## Decision-centered

Every major subsystem exists to support the investment decision lifecycle.

---

## Explainability

Every conclusion should be explainable.

---

## Separation of Concerns

Every subsystem owns a single responsibility.

---

## Provider Independence

Business logic must never depend directly on market-data providers.

---

## AI Assists

AI explains.

AI educates.

AI never replaces business rules.

---

## Immutable History

Evidence snapshots represent historical truth.

Snapshots are never modified.

# Presentation Layer

Contains:

- Dashboard
- Decision Workspace
- Portfolio
- Watchlists
- Reports
- Journal
- Education
- Decision Coach

Purpose:

Present information and collect user interaction.

No business calculations occur here.

---

# Application Layer

Coordinates user workflows.

Examples:

- navigation
- orchestration
- permissions
- request coordination

Business calculations remain inside engines.

---

# Analytical Engines

Specialized engines perform analytical work.

Current engines:

- Market Context Engine
- Market Statistics Engine
- Opportunity Engine
- Setup Detection Engine
- Sector Intelligence Engine
- Evidence Engine
- Decision Confidence Engine

Future engines may be introduced without affecting the overall architecture.

---

# Platform Services

Shared platform capabilities.

Examples:

- Market Data Service
- Alert Service
- Portfolio Service
- Watchlist Service
- AI Orchestration Service
- Authentication
- Evidence Snapshot Service

Platform services support multiple analytical engines.

---

# Shared Symbol Analytical Model

TradeEvidence maintains a canonical symbol-based analytical model.

This model is shared across:

- Dashboard
- Market Context
- Sector Heat Map
- AI Coach
- Alerts
- Reports
- Decision Workspace

This prevents duplicated business logic.

---

# Dashboard Philosophy

The dashboard is a composition layer.

It displays outputs generated by analytical engines.

It does not calculate business logic.

---

# Phase 1

Data source:

CSV

Purpose:

Validate product-market fit.

CSV is an implementation decision.

---

# Phase 2

Data source:

Real-time market providers

The internal architecture remains unchanged.

Only provider adapters evolve.

---

# Provider Abstraction

The application depends on the Market Data Service.

The Market Data Service depends on provider implementations.

Business logic never communicates directly with external providers.

---

# Technology Philosophy

Business logic should remain independent from:

- CSV
- provider SDKs
- databases
- AI models
- deployment platform
